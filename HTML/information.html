Artist of the Month

Arjun Kanungo

Ranks at 181 on leading music platform’s charts

More about artist

Arjun Kanungo as Artist of The Month is Creating waves on a global scale, the singer is known for luring music aficionados with his upbeat music and lyrical ballads that have been setting trends and making waves on the internet.

After his first-ever commercial success ‘Khoon Choos Le’ for the movie ‘Go Goa Gone’, Arjun released some spellbinding singles like, ‘Woh Baarishein’, ‘Sanam Mere Sanam’, ‘Dil Kisi Se’, ‘Aaya Na Tu’, ‘Ek Dafa’, among others, that received immense love from the fans translating into his foremost super hits. The Song ‘Ek Dafa’ is the fusion remix of the legendary composition ‘Chinnama’ that gained him admirable critical acclamation throughout the nation.

Arjun added, “As an artist, the changing dynamics in the industry and newer trends give me an opportunity to not only experiment with a variety of genres but also cater to fans’ ever-changing tastes and preferences. Keeping the same in mind I composed Ek Dafa which is the remix of the popular Chinnamma Chilakkamma. It was for the first time I combined two different tracks and I was overwhelmed by the outcome; the remix has been trending on social media and is loved by many.”


Music History and Culture

Indian music is a rich and diverse tapestry that has evolved over thousands of years, reflecting the country's cultural, social, and religious diversity. From ancient classical traditions to modern fusion experiments, Indian music continues to captivate audiences worldwide.

The origins of music: Music is believed to have originated in prehistoric times, when humans first began to imitate the sounds of nature. The first physical musical instruments date back to over 35,000 years ago.

Ancient music: The first written music dates back to around 3400 BC. Ancient civilizations in Europe, Asia, and Africa all developed their own unique musical traditions.

Medieval music: In Europe, the Middle Ages saw the development of Gregorian chant, a form of religious music that is still sung today. Other popular forms of medieval music included secular songs, such as troubadour ballads, and instrumental music.

Renaissance music: The Renaissance was a time of great cultural and artistic innovation, and music was no exception. This period saw the development of polyphony, a style of music that uses multiple melodies at the same time.

Baroque music: The Baroque period was a time of great opulence and formality, and this is reflected in the music of the time. Baroque composers, such as Bach and Handel, created complex and elaborate works that were designed to impress and awe listeners.

Classical music: The Classical period was a time of balance and refinement in music. Composers such as Mozart and Haydn wrote music that was both elegant and expressive.

Romantic music: The Romantic period was a time of great emotional expression in music. Composers such as Beethoven and Chopin wrote music that was passionate and often dramatic.

20th century music: The 20th century saw a great variety of musical styles emerge, from the experimentalism of Igor Stravinsky to the popular music of the Beatles.

21st century music: The 21st century has seen the continued evolution of musical styles, with new genres such as electronic music and hip-hop becoming increasingly popular.
